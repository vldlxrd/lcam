Art. 09

International Arbitration

A civilised method of resolving commercial and property disputes — an efficient alternative to ordinary courts.

Arbitral tribunals frequently examine disputes over the performance of works, quality of services, penalties or debt recovery, delivering enforceable, recognised awards.

Types of disputes handled through arbitration

  • Corporate disputes
  • Conflicts between founders and shareholders
  • Real-estate disputes (ownership of buildings and land)
  • Construction and contracting disputes
  • Leasing or rental disputes
  • Contractual disputes (cancellations, debts, terminations)
  • Bill-of-exchange and debt-recovery disputes
  • Protection of copyright and intellectual property
  • Defence of reputation, dignity and honour
  • Challenges to decisions of public authorities
  • Enforcement proceedings

Our assistance covers the arbitral procedure in full

  • Advice and risk assessment, identifying amicable solutions
  • Gathering evidence and analysing the opposing party's position
  • Developing legal strategy and case position
  • Filing claims and actions before arbitral tribunals
  • Representing the client at arbitration hearings
  • Assistance with appeals, reviews and enforcement of awards
